About Me

Financial Systems Consultant for Santander UK Technology, Santander UK’s specialist banking software and infrastructure company. More recently I have worked on Santander’s Asset Liability Management (ALM) warehouse which aids the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) division to address the risk faced by the bank due to a mismatch between assets and liabilities. I have been specifically developing a new Interest Rate Risk Management (IRRM) reporting capability to allow the CFO division to monitor and then determine a hedging strategy for the interest rate risk taken on by the funding hub from retail.
Before joining Santander UK Technology, I worked as an Assistant Manager at Ground Coffee Society I worked in one of the busiest environments, the London 2012 Olympics, where I managed and facilitated a team of 20 to help serve up to 3500 customers a day. It was a challenging and very rewarding experience.
I’m truly passionate about building IT solutions in order to solve real world business problems. While I enjoy all aspects of my work, my favourite parts are learning and developing using new programming languages (previous examples are PHP, Javascript, jQuery, HTML5, CSS3, Shell, PL/SQL etc..), as well as truly finding out the business requirement so that I can add value to the client. I constantly look at opportunities to develop and challenge myself.
I have a B.Sc in Information Technology Management for Business (ITMB) from Loughborough University with a 1st Class Honours, as well as being a certified Scrum Master & Developer.

Santander UK Technology

Graduate Trainee Consultant - Financial Systems• July 2015 - July 2016

In July 2015, I started working for Santander UK Technology through their graduate scheme - a scheme which I selected as it enhances my business, technical and management knowledge, something that's very important to me because I always look to develop myself.
Santander UK Technology is Santander’s specialist banking software and infrastructure company, developing software and digital applications, as well as cloud solutions helping to give Santander’s customers a great banking experience.
During the graduate programme, I worked on a number of projects, but the main project I worked on was a decommissioning of a legacy reporting hub, ABB (Abbey Business Banking) Informent, which holds business banking and commercial mortgages information - this information is used to generate internal and external reports for different users, ranging from Risk to Marketing. The reason for decommissioning the reporting hub was that some of the technology used for Informent is unsupported by third parties and internal systems departments, therefore it was decided that Informent would be replaced with Informatica Powercenter 9.6 jobs which would replicate the reports generated by Informent.

Roles: Analysis, Development, Testing, Integration, Project Management
Programming Languages: Control-M, Unix Shell, PL/SQL, Powercenter
